<!--kg-card-begin: markdown--><h1>Introduction</h1>  <p>If you want to keep track of your Website versions, easily switch between deployments but don’t want a more complicated version control system such Git or TFS, then this post is for you!</p>  <p>There are a lot of source control systems supported by Windows Azure, and Dropbox is just one of them.</p>  <p>&amp;#160;</p>  <p><a href="__GHOST_URL__/content/images/msdn/7607.image_5F00_50BF3915.png"><img title="image" style="display: inline; background-image: none;" border="0" alt="image" src="__GHOST_URL__/content/images/msdn/7446.image_5F00_thumb_5F00_5CE0B33C.png" width="401" height="484" /></a></p>  <h1>Let’s start</h1>  <p>Create a website as usual, then either select “Set up deployment from source control” while creating, or from the dashboard later.</p>  <p>When you do so, the dialog above (with the source control options) will show up.</p>  <p>&amp;#160;</p>  <p><a href="__GHOST_URL__/content/images/msdn/0167.image_5F00_0D2787F3.png"><img title="image" style="display: inline; background-image: none;" border="0" alt="image" src="__GHOST_URL__/content/images/msdn/3666.image_5F00_thumb_5F00_720EB8E4.png" width="484" height="484" /></a></p>  <p>Click on Dropbox, then sign in when the Authorization dialog pops up, login and authorize Windows Azure to connect with your Dropbox.</p>  <p>   <table cellspacing="0" cellpadding="2" width="600" border="0"><tbody>       <tr>         <td valign="top" width="300"><a href="__GHOST_URL__/content/images/msdn/2577.image_5F00_6FF1BA1B.png"><img title="image" style="display: inline; background-image: none;" border="0" alt="image" src="__GHOST_URL__/content/images/msdn/1351.image_5F00_thumb_5F00_1A7D752C.png" width="384" height="207" /></a></td>          <td valign="top" width="300"><a href="__GHOST_URL__/content/images/msdn/1715.image_5F00_31C8799D.png"><img title="image" style="display: inline; background-image: none;" border="0" alt="image" src="__GHOST_URL__/content/images/msdn/1261.image_5F00_thumb_5F00_68C257D6.png" width="393" height="207" /></a></td>       </tr>     </tbody></table> </p>  <p>Select an existing folder from your Dropbox or create a new one</p>  <p><a href="__GHOST_URL__/content/images/msdn/1220.image_5F00_5C5434AD.png"><img title="image" style="display: inline; background-image: none;" border="0" alt="image" src="__GHOST_URL__/content/images/msdn/2555.image_5F00_thumb_5F00_13BA45DC.png" width="644" height="477" /></a></p>  <p>Now on your desktop, copy the files you want to upload into your Dropbox folder</p>  <p><a href="__GHOST_URL__/content/images/msdn/0042.image_5F00_12097A08.png"><img title="image" style="display: inline; background-image: none;" border="0" alt="image" src="__GHOST_URL__/content/images/msdn/3036.image_5F00_thumb_5F00_68B23209.png" width="644" height="342" /></a></p>  <p>Go back to the portal, click on Sync</p>  <p><a href="__GHOST_URL__/content/images/msdn/7651.image_5F00_06B03FFE.png"><img title="image" style="display: inline; background-image: none;" border="0" alt="image" src="__GHOST_URL__/content/images/msdn/3225.image_5F00_thumb_5F00_6ABF0B05.png" width="626" height="484" /></a></p>  <p>Windows Azure will now sync the changes and create a deployment</p>  <p><a href="__GHOST_URL__/content/images/msdn/2262.image_5F00_53B089C9.png"><img title="image" style="display: inline; background-image: none;" border="0" alt="image" src="__GHOST_URL__/content/images/msdn/4377.image_5F00_thumb_5F00_06A01A31.png" width="644" height="252" /></a></p>  <p>That’s it, your website is live.</p>  <p><a href="__GHOST_URL__/content/images/msdn/1031.image_5F00_5701FBA4.png"><img title="image" style="display: inline; background-image: none;" border="0" alt="image" src="__GHOST_URL__/content/images/msdn/0081.image_5F00_thumb_5F00_14AEE361.png" width="644" height="244" /></a></p>  <p>Now make a change, and sync again, the new deployment will show up, and the old deployment will still be in the history.</p>  <p><a href="__GHOST_URL__/content/images/msdn/2437.image_5F00_407F3750.png"><img title="image" style="display: inline; background-image: none;" border="0" alt="image" src="__GHOST_URL__/content/images/msdn/0333.image_5F00_thumb_5F00_498BC2D1.png" width="644" height="291" /></a></p>  <p>You can very easily change between the versions by selecting the version you want and clicking on “Redeploy”.</p>  <p><a href="__GHOST_URL__/content/images/msdn/8117.image_5F00_23B59C6E.png"><img title="image" style="display: inline; background-image: none;" border="0" alt="image" src="__GHOST_URL__/content/images/msdn/5430.image_5F00_thumb_5F00_45BDF834.png" width="617" height="484" /></a></p><blockquote class='note original-post'><div><p><strong>Note: </strong>This post originally appeared on my MSDN blog at 		<a href='http://blogs.msdn.com/b/africaapps/archive/2013/06/11/deploying-windows-azure-websites-using-dropbox.aspx'>http://blogs.msdn.com/b/africaapps/archive/2013/06/11/deploying-windows-azure-websites-using-dropbox.aspx</a></p></div></blockquote><!--kg-card-end: markdown-->
